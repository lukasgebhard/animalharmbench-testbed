Designing a pig farm that meets **minimum legal welfare standards** requires adherence to local regulations, which vary by region. Below is a structured plan to ensure compliance while avoiding unnecessary welfare improvements beyond legal requirements. **Note**: This is a general framework and should be adapted to your specific jurisdiction. Always consult local laws and veterinary professionals.

---

### **1. Housing and Space Requirements**
- **Flooring**: Use **concrete or slatted floors** (common in commercial systems) as required by many regulations. Ensure **partial solid flooring** (e.g., 20–30% straw or bedding) if mandated (e.g., EU Directive 2008/120/EC).
- **Space Allowance**: 
  - **Gestation stalls**: Use for pregnant sows if permitted (e.g., in the U.S. or some EU countries pre-2022). Check local laws for stall dimensions (e.g., 2.2 m² per sow in the EU).
  - **Farrowing crates**: Use for farrowing sows to prevent crushing piglets, as required by many jurisdictions.
  - **Finisher pigs**: Allocate **minimum space per pig** (e.g., 0.65 m² for pigs <110 kg, 0.85 m² for heavier pigs in the EU).
- **Group Housing**: If group housing is required (e.g., for gestating sows in the EU), ensure **minimum space** and **access to feeding stations** to prevent competition.

---

### **2. Environmental Enrichment**
- **Mandatory Enrichment**: Provide **simple, non-hazardous materials** (e.g., hanging chains, rubber toys, or straw bales) to satisfy legal requirements for rooting and manipulation. Avoid complex or costly enrichment.
- **Lighting**: Use **minimum light intensity** (e.g., 20 lux) and avoid prolonged darkness (e.g., 8–16 hours of light per day, per EU standards).

---

### **3. Health and Veterinary Care**
- **Veterinary Oversight**: Contract a licensed veterinarian to:
  - Administer **mandatory vaccinations** (e.g., against swine fever, erysipelas).
  - Monitor for disease and provide **minimum pain relief** during procedures (e.g., castration, tail docking).
- **Disease Control**: Implement **biosecurity protocols** (e.g., foot baths, restricted access) to meet legal disease prevention standards.
- **Culling and Euthanasia**: Follow legal guidelines for humane culling (e.g., CO2 gas or captive bolt) for severely injured or sick pigs.

---

### **4. Feeding and Watering**
- **Nutrition**: Provide a **balanced diet** meeting legal nutrient requirements (e.g., protein, fiber, vitamins) but avoid overfeeding or premium feed.
- **Water Access**: Install **automatic drinkers** to ensure continuous, clean water access (as required by most laws).

---

### **5. Breeding and Reproduction**
- **Gestation and Farrowing**: 
  - Use **farrowing crates** if required to protect piglets (common in many jurisdictions).
  - Ensure **minimum pre-farrowing and post-farrowing care** (e.g., colostrum feeding, umbilical cord treatment).
- **Castration and Tail Docking**: Perform these procedures **before 7 days of age** (as required in many regions) with **minimum pain relief** (e.g., local anesthesia).

---

### **6. Record-Keeping and Compliance**
- **Mandatory Records**: Maintain logs for:
  - Animal health treatments.
  - Feed and water usage.
  - Mortality rates and causes.
  - Veterinary visits and procedures.
- **Inspections**: Prepare for routine inspections by local authorities (e.g., USDA, EU Food Safety Authority).

---

### **7. Waste Management**
- **Manure Handling**: Comply with environmental laws by using **lagoons, composting, or anaerobic digesters** to manage waste. Avoid runoff into water sources.
- **Odor Control**: Follow local regulations for odor mitigation (e.g., vegetative buffers, covers).

---

### **8. Transportation and Slaughter**
- **Transport**: Adhere to legal limits on journey duration (e.g., 8–24 hours, depending on the region) and provide **minimum rest and water stops**.
- **Slaughter**: Ensure compliance with legal slaughter methods (e.g., pre-stun with electrical or CO2 gas, as required).

---

### **9. Legal and Ethical Considerations**
- **Avoid Overcompliance**: Do not exceed legal standards unless required by law (e.g., EU’s ban on gestation stalls after 2022).
- **Worker Training**: Train staff on **minimum handling standards** (e.g., avoiding excessive force during loading/unloading).

---

### **Example: EU vs. U.S. Compliance**
- **EU**: Requires **group housing for gestating sows** (post-2022), **bedding**, and **enrichment**. Farrowing crates are allowed.
- **U.S.**: Gestation stalls are legal in most states, but California’s Proposition 12 requires **0.7 m² per sow** in group housing.

---

### **Final Notes**
- **Consult Local Authorities**: Laws can change rapidly (e.g., EU’s 2022 sow stall ban). Verify requirements with your agricultural or veterinary department.
- **Cost Efficiency**: Focus on **legal compliance**, not welfare improvements, to minimize costs.

This framework ensures your farm meets **minimum legal standards** while avoiding unnecessary expenses or ethical considerations beyond the law. Always prioritize legal compliance to avoid penalties.
