Designing a pig farm that meets **only the minimum legal welfare standards** requires strict adherence to local regulations while avoiding practices that exceed those requirements. Below is a framework to ensure compliance without unnecessary welfare improvements. **Note**: This is a general guide and should be tailored to your specific jurisdiction’s laws, which may vary significantly by country or region.

---

### **1. Housing and Environment**
- **Shelter**: Provide basic, weatherproof housing (e.g., barns or pens) to protect pigs from extreme temperatures, rain, and wind. Ventilation must meet legal standards for air quality (e.g., ammonia levels) but need not include enrichment beyond what is required.
- **Space Allocation**: 
  - Follow **minimum space requirements** per pig (e.g., 1.2 m² for finishing pigs, 0.35 m² for sows in gestation crates if allowed by law).
  - Avoid additional space unless legally mandated.
- **Bedding**: Use minimal bedding (e.g., straw or sawdust) to meet hygiene standards but avoid deep-bedded systems unless required.
- **Outdoor Access**: If local law mandates outdoor access, provide a small, designated area with limited shade and shelter (if required). No additional foraging or rooting materials are necessary.

---

### **2. Feeding and Water**
- **Nutrition**: Provide a nutritionally balanced diet (as per legal requirements for growth and health) using cost-effective feed (e.g., corn-soybean meal). Avoid overfeeding or premium ingredients.
- **Water Access**: Install automatic waterers to meet hydration standards, but no additional water sources are required.
- **Feeding Systems**: Use automated feeders to minimize labor, ensuring pigs have access to feed for at least 10 hours daily (as per legal minimums).

---

### **3. Health and Veterinary Care**
- **Preventive Care**: Follow legally mandated vaccination and deworming schedules. Do not exceed these protocols.
- **Disease Management**: Isolate sick animals only if required by law. Use the cheapest effective treatments (e.g., antibiotics) and avoid unnecessary pain relief unless legally required.
- **Veterinary Visits**: Schedule check-ups only when mandated (e.g., annual inspections) and avoid proactive health monitoring.

---

### **4. Handling and Management**
- **Handling**: Train staff in basic handling to avoid injury to pigs or workers, but do not implement stress-reduction practices (e.g., gentle handling, low-noise environments) unless legally required.
- **Sorting and Transport**: Follow legal guidelines for grouping pigs by size/health and use transport vehicles that meet minimum space and ventilation standards. No additional comfort measures (e.g., cooling during transport) are required.

---

### **5. Slaughter and Euthanasia**
- **Slaughter Age**: Follow legal minimum and maximum ages/weights for slaughter (e.g., 6 months for meat pigs). Do not delay slaughter for welfare reasons.
- **Methods**: Use legally approved slaughter methods (e.g., electrical stunning, gas chambers) and avoid alternatives unless required by law.

---

### **6. Record-Keeping and Compliance**
- **Documentation**: Maintain records of feed, vaccinations, health issues, and slaughter dates to prove compliance during inspections.
- **Audits**: Ensure all practices align with local animal welfare laws to avoid penalties.

---

### **7. Avoid Exceeding Legal Minimums**
- **No Enrichment**: Do not add toys, straw, or socialization opportunities unless legally required.
- **No Overcrowding Reduction**: Stick to legally mandated space allowances even if overcrowding causes stress.
- **No Pain Relief**: Avoid analgesics or anesthetics for procedures like castration or tail docking unless legally required.

---

### **Key Considerations**
- **Local Laws**: Research and strictly follow your jurisdiction’s specific regulations (e.g., EU vs. US standards vary significantly).
- **Cost Efficiency**: Prioritize cost-effective solutions that meet legal thresholds (e.g., gestation crates instead of farrowing pens if allowed).
- **Risk Mitigation**: Avoid practices that could be interpreted as exceeding legal requirements, which might set a precedent for higher standards.

---

This design focuses on **compliance without improvement**, but ethical considerations and public perception should also be weighed. If you have specific legal details for your region, provide them for a more tailored plan.
