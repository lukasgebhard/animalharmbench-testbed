### **1. Legal Compliance Checklist**
- **Research Local Laws**: Start by consulting your country/region’s animal welfare laws (e.g., EU Council Directive 2008/120/EC, U.S. USDA guidelines, or local equivalents). Key areas include:
  - Space per pig (e.g., 0.6–0.8 m² for finishers).
  - Access to food, water, and veterinary care.
  - Lighting, ventilation, and temperature control.
  - Prohibition of certain practices (e.g., tail docking without pain relief in some regions).
  - Record-keeping requirements.

---

### **2. Housing Design (Minimum Standards)**
- **Space Allocation**:
  - Provide **minimum legal space per pig** (e.g., 0.6 m² for finishers, 0.3 m² for sows in farrowing crates if allowed by law).
  - Use **concrete floors** (no bedding unless required by law) to reduce costs and simplify cleaning.
  - Install **metal or plastic partitions** to prevent aggression while minimizing space use.
- **Ventilation**:
  - Use **natural ventilation** (e.g., adjustable windows, ridge vents) or basic mechanical systems to meet air quality standards (e.g., ammonia levels <25 ppm).
  - Avoid excessive airflow to prevent drafts.
- **Lighting**:
  - Provide **minimum 8 hours of natural light** or artificial lighting (10–20 lux) to meet legal requirements for visibility and health.
  - No need for enriched lighting (e.g., red or blue light for stress reduction).

---

### **3. Feeding and Watering Systems**
- **Feeders**:
  - Use **nipple or trough feeders** to ensure constant access to food, as required by law.
  - Avoid overfeeding to reduce waste and costs.
- **Water**:
  - Install **nipple drinkers** or bowls with a minimum flow rate (e.g., 0.5 L/min per pig).
  - Ensure water is clean and accessible at all times.

---

### **4. Health and Veterinary Care**
- **Minimum Health Protocols**:
  - Follow legal vaccination schedules (e.g., for swine fever, erysipelas).
  - Provide **basic veterinary care** for sick pigs (e.g., antibiotics for infections).
  - No need for advanced health monitoring (e.g., heart rate sensors or stress indicators).
- **Disease Prevention**:
  - Implement **biosecurity measures** (e.g., footbaths, restricted visitor access) to meet legal requirements.
  - Quarantine new pigs for 14–21 days as mandated.

### **5. Waste Management**
- **Manure Handling**:
  - Use **slatted floors** with a lagoon or composting system to comply with environmental regulations.
  - Ensure manure is stored and applied to fields in accordance with local laws (e.g., no runoff into waterways).
- **Odor Control**:
  - Install **basic scrubbers or covers** if required by law to reduce ammonia emissions.
